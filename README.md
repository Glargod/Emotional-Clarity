Redefining Emotional ResponsesOverviewThis project clarifies "Sympathy," "Empathy," and introduces "Koinpathy" to address ambiguity in emotional terminology. Authored by Robert Charest, with assistance from AI models Grok and Gemini.Problem"Sympathy" and "Empathy" are often conflated, hindering precise communication in psychology, sociology, and ethics.ConfusionHistorical evolution and modern splits have blurred their meanings, leading to misunderstandings in clinical and societal contexts.Solution
Sympathy: Self-emotional response to an outside event.
Empathy: Awareness of an outside emotion created by an event.
Koinpathy: Shared, mirrored emotion driving collective action.
ImplicationsEnhances clarity in therapy, policy, and relationships by distinguishing these terms.AuthorRobert CharestContributorsGrok and GeminiLicense
